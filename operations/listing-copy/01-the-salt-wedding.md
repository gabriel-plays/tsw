## Long description (product page)

One hundred years ago, in the manor of Mire End, the wedding of Lady Vesna and Baron Aldric was preserved in salt at the moment of the vow. The bride is still standing. So are the guests. So is the priest, mid-blessing, his book turned to the same page. The bells ring tonight without ringers. The salt is beginning to crack.

*The Salt Wedding* is a system-neutral one-night adventure for 3–5 characters of modest ability, designed to run in a single ~3-hour session. It contains:

- A frozen manor with eight keyed locations
- Six NPCs whose lives have been held mid-breath for a century
- Five honest endings, including one that does not require a fight
- A hedge-witch boy in the cellar who has been weeping since the centennial began
- Rules for *manor-salt*: a substance that stops bleeding, holds back what is held back, and binds a vow to the tongue
- A d6 rumor table for pre-adventure setup
- A clock mechanism for pacing the night

Compatible with Mörk Borg, Cairn, Shadowdark, Old-School Essentials, Mausritter, and any system that lets a wedding be sad. No stat conversion needed.

This adventure is about being asked to choose for someone who cannot choose for themselves, and recognizing you do not get to.
