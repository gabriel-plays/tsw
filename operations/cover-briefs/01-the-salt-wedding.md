# The Salt Wedding — Cover Brief

For commission ($15–25 on Fiverr — search "fantasy book cover illustration moody"). For AI generation, paste into your tool of choice and iterate. The cover is the single highest-leverage variable in DTRPG conversion — do not let it be the bottleneck, but do not phone it in either.

---

## Subject

A bride in a high-collared wedding dress, standing at the head of a long oak banquet table, frozen mid-motion. Her eyes are slightly open and *aware*. Salt has drifted across the floor and crept up her dress like frost. Candles burn upright behind her, untouched by time. Slight tilt to the camera, as if from the perspective of a guest looking up.

## Mood

Grim, beautiful, uneasy. Vesna is not a monster. She is a girl who has been awake too long. The viewer should want to look away and not be able to.

## Color palette

Deep blacks and cold whites. Candlelight as the only warmth. Slight blue-grey shift in the salt. Avoid saturation. The cover should look like it could be a hundred years old itself.

## Style references

- Zdzisław Beksiński's quiet portraits
- The *Mörk Borg* aesthetic without the metal
- Illustrations from *Pan's Labyrinth*
- Andrew Wyeth's *Christina's World*

Aim for hand-drawn or painted feel — the cover is the single highest-leverage variable in DTRPG conversion and an obvious AI image will be downvoted in the comments within hours.

## What to avoid

- Glowing magical effects
- Visible monster faces
- Sword-and-sorcery clichés
- Six fingers, melted faces in background, gibberish text — any AI hand-tells

## Backup if commission falls through

Use a public-domain salt landscape (the Bonneville Salt Flats from Internet Archive, desaturated to monochrome) with hand-set black title text in a serif face — Cormorant Garamond or EB Garamond is free and appropriate. Strong, restrained, type-driven covers actually outperform mediocre illustrations in the OSR niche. Do not let the commission be the bottleneck.

## Specifications

- Final size: 1500 × 2400 px minimum (DTRPG product cover spec)
- Format: JPG or PNG
- Title placement: top third, IM Fell English or similar archaic serif
- Series banner: bottom (small caps, "THE MIRE END CYCLE · NO. I")
- Bleed: not required for digital-only release
